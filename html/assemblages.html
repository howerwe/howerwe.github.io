Our work on objects
Assemblages

Assemblages are a collection of objects that must be stored, moved or handled together to form an item. Predominantly in archaeology, an assemblage could include a large number of related objects.

Details of individual items in the assembly are underneath the item or inscribed onto the item, difficult to read/interpret.

Assemblages cannot be digitalised with photography alone, as there are many minute details on the objects that can be difficult to capture via photography alone. By utilising a 3D flatbed scanner, such as the LUTRACAD Flatbed, details all around the object can be captured, which is important as it allows for viewers to have a more interactive experience. By taking several photographs of the object, it is able to build a 3D model like the one you see on the left! Furthermore, our trained volunteers will work alongside AI identification systems to verify automated classifications and add contextual information that the scanners may have missed.

In total, we believe that to digitise all assemblages it would take approximately 28 months with a cost of $500,000 AUD.

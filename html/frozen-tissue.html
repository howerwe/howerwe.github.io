Frozen Tissue Objects

Our digital objects are frozen tissue sections, typically prepared from animal or human organs (e.g., mouse liver, human cerebral cortex) using a –80 °C cryostat. The sections are cut to a thickness of 10–20 µm, preserving cellular and tissue microstructures for downstream histology, pathology, and spatial-omics research.

The entire imaging workflow must be maintained at –80 °C ± 2 °C; even brief warming will cause tissue thawing, structural deformation, or ice-crystal artifacts. We use a dedicated chilled-stage and handle slides with insulated tools or a low-temperature robotic arm to minimize sample exposure.

Even with rapid freezing in liquid-nitrogen–cooled isopentane, residual moisture can condense into ice crystals during handling. We employ a dry-nitrogen purge or inert-gas blanket over the imaging area, and equip objectives with anti-fog coatings to maintain clear optics.

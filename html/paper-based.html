Paper Based Objects

Paper Based Objects include bound books, loose documents, journals, articles, newspaper segments and art. Most of these objects are in the Archives collection, however natural sciences and archaeology have a small proportion.

These objects tend to be quite fragile and eroded due to old preservation.

Our solution for paper based objects takes inspiration from the ALICE system that the Natural History Museum in London uses. It takes multiple pictures at the same time and then reads the text from labels automatically to create digital records. 800 specimens can be digitised each day through this method, allowing rapid digitization, detailed record-keeping, and preservation of delicate labels.

It’ll take approximately 28 months to complete digitisation of all paper based objects with it costing about $14,500 AUD.

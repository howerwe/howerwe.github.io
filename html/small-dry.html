Our work on objects
Small Dry Objects

Small Dry Objects include freely handled coins, beads, pottery fragments including some bearing labels or inscriptions.

These objects have very high shape/material variability requires consistent framing. Labels and inscriptions may be faint or worn, demanding high-resolution capture and image correction.

These images are automated coin/object recognition on AWS: 11 SageMaker models classify obverse/reverse images in seconds.

Similar to assemblages small dry objects cannot be digitalised with photography alone, as there are many minute details on the objects that can be difficult to capture via photography alone. By utilising a 3D flatbed scanner, such as the LUTRACAD Flatbed, details all around the object can be captured, which is important as it allows for viewers to have a more interactive experience. Furthermore, our trained volunteers will work alongside AI identification systems to verify automated classifications and add contextual information that the scanners may have missed.

The time required to digitise all of these objects are approximately and will cost about $184,000 AUD.

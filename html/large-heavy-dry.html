Our work on objects
Large/Heavy Dry Objects

Large and heavy objects include historical storage cabinets, and cultural artifacts. These items are particularly challenging due to their weight and fragility and cannot be frequently moved without risk of damage, requiring specialized imaging spaces and careful handling protocols. In order to ensure this we propose implementing a mobile 3D photogrammetry rig - a portable framework equipped with multiple high-resolution cameras and professional lighting systems. The California PLOS Journal states that the rig is a system that captures hundreds of overlapping photographs from all angles and then transforms them into a 3D model. These digital models allow researchers and the public to explore artifacts virtually, providing unprecedented access without any physical interaction or risk to the original objects. To meet location tracking requirements, we have taken the CPCON groups research on Radio Frequency Identification as our implementation method. This technology is a mature and highly effective wireless identification solution that uses radio waves to track and identify objects through its core components: tags, readers, and backend systems. Large objects require specialized handling by professionals, but volunteers will contribute by researching and adding historical context to digital records.

These objects takes a large amount of space and need for special handling/ equipment.

We will use the Mobile 3D photogrammetry rig - portable framework with multiple high resolution cameras and professional lighting systems to digitise Large/Heavy Dry Objects.

It’ll take approximately 12 months and about $2,020,000 AUD to fully digitise all Large/Heavy Dry Objects.
